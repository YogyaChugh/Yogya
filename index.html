About

essay background 06 min read

I I started writing software in high school because the games I wanted didn't exist yet — so I made crude ones. Five years later that habit grew teeth: I'm writing a Rust IDE that emits classes.dex by hand, a C++ DOM that follows the WhatWG spec to the letter, and the kind of client software where someone actually depends on the uptime.

Most of what I've learned came from shipping. Hack Club's Summer of Making put 450 hours on the clock in three months — four open-source projects out the door, a laptop on the desk. The international hackathons at Derby and Ulster taught me that 36 hours is enough to do something real with a small team if you stop arguing about frameworks.

I'm in my 3rd year of B.Tech CSE in Delhi (CGPA 8.38, zero backlogs, two semesters left). I read specs for fun, I lose at online chess, and I keep building games I have no business playing.

Looking for an SDE internship where I can write a lot of code that ships and learn from people who care about craft. ↳ Hit ⌘K to say hi.
